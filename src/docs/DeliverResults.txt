"Let me share an experience from my time at Apple's Device Service team where I not only met but exceeded expectations while handling a critical API migration project.
Situation: In October 2024, I was tasked with migrating 30 APIs from a legacy service (Raven) to a new microservice architecture. These APIs were crucial for device activation and management, directly impacting our customers' ability to use their Apple devices.

Task: My goal was to complete the migration by February 2025. The challenge was significant because these APIs were business-critical, and any errors could disrupt device activation for customers worldwide. Additionally, we had limited documentation as the original developers had left the company.

Action: Demonstrating customer obsession and ownership, I:

Took a systematic approach by first understanding the customer impact through UI investigation and Chrome developer tools
Organized the 30 APIs into 6 logical groups based on functionality to ensure methodical testing and deployment
Dove deep into the codebase to understand every nuance of the implementation
Collaborated closely with QA to establish comprehensive testing protocols
Initiated direct customer feedback sessions to ensure the migration met user needs
Built automated comparison tools to verify new API responses matched legacy behavior
Proactively identified and addressed potential risks before they could impact customers
Result: I exceeded expectations by:

Completing the migration ahead of schedule by January 2025
Achieving zero customer-reported issues post-deployment
Implementing additional monitoring and alerting systems beyond the original scope
Creating detailed documentation for future maintenance
Receiving positive feedback from user acceptance testing
Successfully passing all performance and stress tests in staging
Ensuring zero service disruptions during the transition
